Human Centered Design in Software Engineering

Alimi Eyitayo TBD, 2021
9:00 AM - 12:30 PM (MDT)

In this workshop you will learn how to apply Domain-Driven Design in an environment with a dominant legacy system.

How might we make an indispensable product out of a painful problem? Who's going to be there and what is going to be the desired outcome?

Human-Centred Design (HCD) is the collection of different methods and approaches to bring out the discipline of developing solutions in the service of people. Introducing these design methods in software development essentially helps you design like a user for a user and not design like a developer for the user which is a miss-match and leads to creating a huge user-product divide.

WHAT YOU WILL LEARN

This workshop has been organized by way of three key design skills: Looking, understanding and making.

Looking:

  • Innovation begins and ends with people which calls for keen and caring observation.
    • You will learn how to use HCD to carefully investigate if there is a need for the product being planned.
      • How might you use curiosity, objectivity and empathy to engage all of your senses in pursuit of meaningful findings?

Understanding:

  • Innovation isn't a lightbulb moment of genius. It calls for deep understanding and rigorous discernment.
    • You will learn how to use HCD to revolutionize critical thinking and problem framing.
      • How might you identify patterns, determine priorities, and translate your research into actionable insights?

Making:

  • Innovation puts great ideas in action. It calls for making things happen in a resourceful and resonant manner.
    • You will learn how to incorporate imaginative and visual expression in HCD to precedes successful improvement through frequent iteration.
      • How might you think with your hands to bring new ideas to life?

WORKSHOP FORMAT

Interactive learnings and group assignments.

WORKSHOP SCHEDULE

Day 1 - Looking

Methods of observing human experience.

Understanding ethnographic, participatory and evaluative research and how HCD can be leveraged for:

  • Stepping out of your native environment to learn what people do in their abode to spur Innovation.
  • Paying close attention to discover critical and latent needs to offer solutions people don't even know they need.
  • Assessing the usefulness and usability of the solutions meant to serve people.

Day 2 - Understanding

Methods for analysing challenges and opportunities.

Understanding the relationship between people & systems, pattern & priorities and problem framing to ensure accuracy of:

  • Understanding the subject area to transcend and sometimes pivot the solution to serve different people at different places.
  • Encouraging clear and critical thinking which is essential for discerning what is related, what is essential and what is important.
  • Characterizing the problem with invaluable methods. To be an effective problem solver, you need to be a creative problem framer

    • Day 3 - Making

    Methods for envisioning future possibilities.

    Using HCD to produce a game-changing innovation by merging concept ideation, modelling & prototyping and design rationale.

    • Make use of concept ideation as a good and engaging alternative conventional brainstorming techniques.
    • Using modelling and prototyping to bring new ideas to life while taking a controlled risk.
    • Many good ideas fail to become innovative because the creators fail to convey the concept’s full potential.

    Learn how to leverage HCD to help compel others to take the necessary actions for great ideas to flourish.
